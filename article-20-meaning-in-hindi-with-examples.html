Article 20 Meaning in Hindi With Examples¬† Article 20 Kya Hai? (2025 Guide)

Short Summary

Article 20 Indian Constitution ka ek fundamental right hai jo criminals ke rights ko protect karta hai. Isme teen main protections hoti hain: ex-post-facto laws, double jeopardy, aur self-incrimination se protection.

¬†Article 20 Meaning in Hindi (Hinglish Explanation)

Article 20 ek bahut important constitutional right hai jo kisi bhi accused person ko unfair treatment se bachata hai. Yeh Article ensure karta hai ki kisi bhi criminal case mein government ya police accused ke basic rights ko violate na kare.

¬†Article 20 Kya Hai? (Simple Hinglish Meaning)

Article 20 Indian Constitution ka wo part hai jo criminal offenses aur punishments se related protections deta hai.

Is Article ke according kisi bhi vyakti ke saath tez, fair aur just legal process follow hona chahiye.

Article 20 Ke Teen Main Provisions

¬†1. Protection Against Ex-Post-Facto Law

Iska matlab hai ki kisi bhi insaan ko us act ke liye punish nahi kiya ja sakta jo us time crime nahi tha.
Agar baad mein law change hota hai, fir bhi punishment piche se apply nahi hoti.

Example:

Agar 2015 mein ek activity legal thi aur 2017 mein illegal ban gayi, to person ko 2015 ki action ke liye punish nahi kiya ja sakta.

2. Protection Against Double Jeopardy

Yeh principle kehta hai ki ek hi offense ke liye kisi person ko do baar punish nahi kiya ja sakta.

Example:

Agar Rohan ko theft case mein court ne pehle hi acquit/dismiss kar diya hai, to police usi theft ke liye fir se case nahi kar sakti.

¬†3. Protection Against Self-Incrimination

Iska matlab hai ki accused ko apne khilaf khud gavaahi dene ke liye force nahi kiya ja sakta.

Example:

Police kisi person ko torture ya pressure dekar confession nahi karwa sakti.
Accused ko chup rehne ka right hota hai (Right to Silence).

H2: Article 20 Ka Objective

Article 20 ka main objective yeh hai ki kisi bhi criminal case mein accused ko fair protection, due process aur dignity mile.
Isse law system zyada transparent aur human-friendly banta hai.

Q1: Article 20 kya protect karta hai?

Article 20 accused person ke rights protect karta hai‚Äîex-post-facto law, double jeopardy, self-incrimination.

Q2: Kya Article 20 sabhi cases mein apply hota hai?

Nahi, yeh sirf criminal proceedings mein apply hota hai, civil cases mein nahi.

Q3: Kya Self-Incrimination ka protection DNA fingerprints par apply hota hai?

Nahi, physical evidence (DNA, fingerprints) Article 20(3) mein protected nahi hai.

Conclusion

Article 20 India ke Constitution ka ek strong protective measure hai jo accused persons ke rights ko secure karta hai. Yeh ensure karta hai ki kisi bhi vyakti ko bina fair trial ke punish na kiya ja sake.
